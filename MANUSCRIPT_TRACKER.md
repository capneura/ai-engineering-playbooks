## Chapter Workflow

Each chapter should move through the following stages:

1. Evidence extraction
2. Principle extraction
3. M0.1 draft
4. Review
5. M0.2 revision
6. Freeze
7. Commit
8. Move forward

## Freeze Rule

A chapter is considered frozen only when:

- Its core argument is clear.
- Its examples are generalized.
- It does not name the source project.
- Its recommendations answer the four editorial questions.
- It has a practical checklist.
- It has common mistakes.
- It has engineering laws.
- It has been committed with a clear message.

Frozen does not mean perfect.

Frozen means stable enough to build on.

## Standard Chapter Sections

Most chapters should contain most of the following:

- Mission Brief
- Core Principle
- Real Engineering Experience
- Evidence
- Engineering Rule
- Failure Pattern
- Field Notes
- Key Takeaways
- Practical Checklist
- Common Mistakes
- Engineering Laws

## Editorial Questions

Every major recommendation must answer:

1. What happened?
2. What was learned?
3. What engineering rule emerged?
4. How can another builder apply it?

## Evidence Spine

The manuscript draws from recurring engineering experience, including:

- Project drift
- Mixing planning with implementation
- Loss of architectural decisions
- Importance of summaries
- Transition from opinion to telemetry
- Canonical sources of truth
- Layered authority
- Debugging authority instead of symptoms
- Scope freezing
- Incremental repair
- Verification before redesign
- Recovering context across long-running work
- Preserving collaboration quality with AI over many sessions
